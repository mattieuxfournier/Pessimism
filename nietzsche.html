Friedrich Nietzsche

1844-1900

German philosopher known for his critiques of traditional morality and religion

Key Aphorisms

"To live is to suffer, to survive is to find some meaning in the suffering."

"He who has a why to live can bear almost any how."

"You must have chaos within you to give birth to a dancing star."

Philosophical Ideas

  • Übermensch (Superman) concept
  • Will to power
  • Eternal recurrence
  • "God is dead" proclamation
  • Master-slave morality

Biography

Friedrich Nietzsche was born in 1844 in Prussia. He became a professor of classical philology at the University of Basel at just 24 years old. Health problems forced him to resign, after which he lived as an independent philosopher, producing his most famous works including "Thus Spoke Zarathustra" and "Beyond Good and Evil."

Nietzsche suffered a mental collapse in 1889 and spent his final years in care until his death in 1900. His sister Elisabeth later edited and distorted his works to align with her nationalist ideology, leading to much misinterpretation of his philosophy.

Legacy

Nietzsche is among the most influential philosophers of modern times. His ideas on power, morality, and the death of God profoundly impacted existentialism, postmodernism, and psychoanalysis. Thinkers like Martin Heidegger, Michel Foucault, and Jacques Derrida engaged deeply with his work.

Despite being appropriated by the Nazis (against his actual philosophy), Nietzsche's work continues to challenge conventional thinking across philosophy, psychology, and cultural studies. His concept of self-overcoming remains particularly relevant in contemporary discussions of human potential.
