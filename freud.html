Sigmund Freud

1856-1939

Founder of psychoanalysis and explorer of the unconscious mind

Key Aphorisms

"Most people do not really want freedom, because freedom involves responsibility, and most people are frightened of responsibility."

"The first human who hurled an insult instead of a stone was the founder of civilization."

"Unexpressed emotions will never die. They are buried alive and will come forth later in uglier ways."

Philosophical Ideas

  • The unconscious mind
  • Psychosexual development
  • Id, ego, and superego
  • Dream interpretation
  • Defense mechanisms

Biography

Sigmund Freud was born in 1856 in the Moravian town of Freiberg (now Czech Republic) to Jewish parents. He studied medicine at the University of Vienna and established his clinical practice there, where he developed psychoanalysis. Freud fled to London following Nazi Germany's annexation of Austria, where he died in 1939.

Freud's early work focused on neurology before shifting to psychology. His development of talk therapy and theories about childhood sexuality were controversial but revolutionized the understanding of human behavior.

Legacy

Freud's theories, though many have been challenged or disproven, fundamentally changed how we think about the human mind. Concepts like the unconscious, repression, and Freudian slips remain part of popular culture. His emphasis on childhood experiences shaped modern psychology and education.

While psychoanalysis as a therapeutic technique has declined, Freud's influence persists in literary theory, gender studies, and cultural criticism. Thinkers like Jacques Lacan built upon his work, and his ideas continue to spark debate in academic circles.
